# Math1_FA1_FA2


Introduction to the Project

Cryptocurrency markets are well known for experiencing extreme price volatility, and it is difficult for a beginner or a student to fully grasp the concept of how prices are changing in the markets. The prices of digital assets, such as Bitcoin, are constantly changing, and there are many factors that contribute to this, such as market sentiment, speculation, and news from the global economy, among many others. The sudden changes in prices are normally termed market volatility, and it is difficult for a beginner or a student to fully grasp how the markets are behaving.

This project aims to analyze the volatility in the prices of cryptocurrencies by designing a project known as a Crypto Volatility Visualizer, where a simulation is created to show how different mathematical patterns affect the markets. Unlike real data, the project utilizes mathematical patterns such as sine waves, cosine waves, and random noise to simulate the markets, where some patterns are predictable, and others are unpredictable, just like in real markets.

The purpose of the project is to develop a simple yet interactive tool to visualize the concept of volatility in the market. The parameters can be adjusted to show the impact of different market conditions on the patterns of price movement in the stock market. The user can thus learn the difference between a stable market and a volatile one.

The project has been designed into two phases: Formative Assessment 1 (FA-1) deals with the ideation, research, and planning of the design of the system, including the identification of the problem, the identification of the persona of the user, and the design of the interface of the system. Formative Assessment 2 (FA-2) focuses on implementing the idea by developing an application that simulates the movement of cryptocurrency prices using different mathematical models.

In the project, the concepts of mathematical modeling, data visualization, and basic AI have been brought together to show the user the application of such computational tools in the analysis of complex systems in the stock market.


FA-1: Ideation and Planning Phase
During this phase, FA-1 concentrated on planning and designing the concept of the Crypto Volatility Visualizer before developing the application. The major problem identified during FA-1 was that the market for cryptocurrencies is highly volatile and hard to understand for beginners. To address this, a storyboard has been developed that shows how an interactive tool can help users understand the market

FA-2: Implementation Phase

In FA-2, the idea and design created in FA-1 were implemented in a working web application called Crypto Volatility Visualizer. The objective of FA-2 was to turn the designed idea into an interactive system where a user can interactively investigate cryptocurrency market volatility using mathematical simulations.

The application was created using Python and Streamlit, which allowed for the creation of an interactive web-based interface. Several parameters can be adjusted, such as frequency, amplitude, drift, and random noise, which affect how the simulated price changes behave. These parameters represent different market conditions where a user can compare how stable markets behave in comparison to unstable ones.

In order to produce the simulated price patterns, the application utilizes mathematical functions such as sine waves, cosine waves, and stochastic noise components. The functions are useful in simulating both predictable and unpredictable price patterns, which are common in cryptocurrency markets. The application updates graphs that display the price patterns as the parameters are altered.

The dashboard interface enables the user to test different patterns and compare the results. The interactive simulation is useful in helping learners understand how volatility operates and how different factors affect it.

The fully functional application is available at the following link:

In conclusion, FA-2 has been successful in transforming the conceptual design from FA-1 into a fully functional interactive simulation application that demonstrates cryptocurrency volatility through mathematical modeling and data representation.

Reflection on FA-2 Development

The FA-2 application was developed in partnership with my classmate. The two of us collaborated in order to transform the concept developed in FA-1 into a working web application. During the development of the application, we decided to divide the workload in order to efficiently develop the application. The two of us worked on the mathematical simulation logic and the creation of price patterns, and the other part of the workload was on the creation of the interactive interface and testing the application.

We also collaborated in fine-tuning the parameters, including amplitude, frequency, drift, and noise, in order to ensure that the price patterns developed could realistically reflect various levels of market volatility. The development of the application was successful, and we have been able to create a working web application known as the Crypto Volatility Visualizer, which showcases how mathematical models can be utilized in simulating cryptocurrency market behavior.
